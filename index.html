“Self-initiated cover redesign for Replica 2.”

Replica 2 is a collaborative project between rapper Huntrill and beatmaker/producer Hologram Lo'. The album deals with money, status and street culture.

The cover is built around a single image — a modified dollar bill. The face is blurred and the design altered to represent money that isn't legitimate. On the back cover, a pink bar censors George Washington, a figure that appears on real dollars and here on Huntrill's counterfeit version.

“A collective built around circular motion and underground culture.”

Spiral Collectif is a Bordeaux-based creative collective. The visual identity is built around the spiral as both symbol and system — a form that suggests movement, repetition, and depth.

Just Garbage But Fun To Make is the debut album by TAM, second member of the collective. The cover places him in a snow field surrounded by a handwritten typographic system, with the Spiral logo anchoring the bottom right — raw, lo-fi, and deliberate.

“One character, infinite universes — each appearance is a new world.”

DOXAL is my alter ego — a TV-headed figure that exists across a multiverse. Every time he appears, it's a new universe with a new visual style: ink on paper, photo collage, digital glitch, backrooms, Moroccan sky. Same character, different reality.

The project has no fixed medium and no fixed aesthetic. The only constant is the figure itself — and the cable that follows him everywhere.

“Dog food delivery — but make it a brand you actually want to wear.”

SWOOF is a dog food delivery brand built around energy, speed and personality. The identity takes inspiration from street culture — graffiti lettering, bold colours and a mascot system that makes the brand immediately recognisable.

The logo, packaging and merch all speak the same language: loud, fun, and built to move fast.
